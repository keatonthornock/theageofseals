World Lore

Open the field notes below to glimpse the vows, peoples, and mysteries behind the trilogy.

The Seals are ancient stewardships entrusted to peoples, places, and guardians. Each preserves a good that cannot survive when treated as possession.

Sentinels are watchmen bound to protect what was given, not rule what was entrusted. Their order has faded into rumor and half-kept ceremony.

Solith is remembered as refuge, song, and burden—a hidden inheritance guarded by the Durnai long after the outer kingdoms stopped listening.

The Durnai are keepers of memory, a people of quiet endurance whose customs preserve truths that official histories buried beneath conquest.

The Heart is the old name for the world’s deepest center of trust, where creation, covenant, and ruin meet in the final accounting.
